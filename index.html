Research



My current research interest lie in the intersection of Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing. I hope to focus within the areas of multi-modal translations, with attention given to low resource languages. I am working on, and have worked on, some exciting projects! Here are my major contributions in fields ranging from biocomputing to my current interests in language technology.

  • Fall 2020 Under the advisory of Jesse Read at the DaSciM team at LIX, I am completing my Bachelor's thesis on Context Aware Cross Lingual Representations for Low Resource Machine Translation.
  • Fall 2020 Under the advisory of Vicky Kalogeiton at the GeoVic team at LIX, I am conduting a research project in Self-supervised learning of Human interactions through sync-tracking.
  • Summer 2020 Under the advisory of Zied Sellami at Linagora, I
  • Spring 2020 Under the advisory of Thomas Simonson at BioC, I developed a Monte-Carlo method to determine characteristic sequence alignments for Tyrosine Kinase proteins.
  • Summer 2019 Under the advisory of Pierre Boyer at CREST, I studied impacts of large scale social media movments (on Twitter) on local legislation. I built tools to track real time tweets and get geolocation data which I then extrapolated into chloropleth maps. Finally, I performed sentiment analysis on bilingual tweets using a Naïve Bayes classifier.
